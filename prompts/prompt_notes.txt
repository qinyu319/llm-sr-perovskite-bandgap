Prompt–M0 (Linear baseline: main effects only)
You are a materials-physics and symbolic-regression expert. We aim to build an interpretable analytical model for the optical band gap Eg (eV) of 3D ABX3 hybrid perovskites.

Input features are composition fractions with clear physical meaning:
Sn (B-site Sn fraction), Br and Cl (X-site halide fractions). Each lies in [0,1].

Task: Propose a strictly linear analytical structure (constant + first-order terms only) to be later fitted on the training set.
Constraints:
1) Allowed terms: {constant, Sn, Br, Cl} only.
2) Do NOT use squared terms, interaction products, ratios, logs, exponentials, or any higher-order terms.
3) Keep the structure minimal and physically interpretable.
4) Do NOT provide any numerical coefficients and do NOT explain.

Output format: output exactly one line, e.g.
Eg = a0 + a1*Sn + a2*Br + a3*Cl
Prompt–M1 (Add single-variable curvature: allow squares, no interactions)
You are a materials-physics and symbolic-regression expert. The linear baseline is insufficient; we want to capture single-variable nonlinearity (curvature) in the band-gap response.

Inputs: Sn, Br, Cl in [0,1].

Task: Propose an interpretable quadratic structure with main effects and squared terms, to be fitted later on the training set.
Constraints:
1) Allowed terms: {constant, Sn, Br, Cl, Sn^2, Br^2, Cl^2}.
2) Do NOT use any interaction/product terms (e.g., Sn*Br), and do NOT use higher powers.
3) Do NOT use ratios, logs, exponentials, or any non-polynomial terms.
4) Keep the structure compact; do not include unnecessary terms.
5) Do NOT provide numerical coefficients and do NOT explain.

Output format: exactly one line, e.g.
Eg = a0 + a1*Sn + a2*Br + a3*Cl + a4*Sn^2 + a5*Br^2 + a6*Cl^2
Prompt–M2 (Add coupling effects: allow pairwise interactions + squares)
You are a materials-physics and symbolic-regression expert. To capture metal–halide coupling and mixed-halide effects, we now allow second-order interaction terms.

Inputs: Sn, Br, Cl in [0,1].

Task: Propose a second-order (quadratic) analytical structure that may include main effects, squared terms, and a small number of physically motivated pairwise interactions, to be fitted later on the training set.

Allowed term pool:
- Main effects: Sn, Br, Cl
- Squares: Sn^2, Br^2, Cl^2
- Pairwise interactions (choose up to 2–3): Sn*Br, Sn*Cl, Br*Cl
- Constant term

Constraints:
1) Use only the term pool above. No higher-order terms, no ratios, no logs, no exponentials.
2) Use at most 2–3 interaction terms; prioritize physically meaningful couplings (metal–halide and mixed-halide).
3) Keep total non-constant terms <= 10.
4) Do NOT provide numerical coefficients and do NOT explain.

Output format: exactly one line, a single expression structure.
Prompt–M3 (Introduce A-site effect: include Cs + selected interactions)
You are a materials-physics and symbolic-regression expert. We now incorporate A-site effects by introducing Cs (A-site Cs fraction in [0,1]) in addition to Sn, Br, Cl.

Inputs: Sn, Br, Cl, Cs in [0,1].

Task: Propose an interpretable quadratic analytical structure that includes Cs main effects and (optionally) Cs-related coupling terms, to be fitted later on the training set.

Allowed terms:
- Main effects: Sn, Br, Cl, Cs
- Squares: Sn^2, Br^2, Cl^2, Cs^2 (optional)
- Interactions: prioritize Cs with B/X terms (Cs*Sn, Cs*Br, Cs*Cl)
- Optionally keep a small number of {Sn*Br, Sn*Cl, Br*Cl} if physically justified
- Constant term

Constraints:
1) Only quadratic terms (main, squares, pairwise products). No higher-order, ratios, logs, exponentials.
2) Total interaction terms <= 4; prioritize Cs-coupling terms first.
3) Keep the structure compact (target <= 12 non-constant terms).
4) Do NOT provide numerical coefficients and do NOT explain.

Output format: exactly one line, a single expression structure.
Prompt–M4 (Final simplification: minimal complexity with near-best accuracy)
You are a symbolic-regression and materials-physics expert. We already have a relatively expressive quadratic model using Sn/Br/Cl/Cs (main effects + squares + a few interactions). Now we want the final recommended formula: simplify the structure as much as possible while maintaining near-optimal predictive performance (to be verified on a held-out test set).

Inputs: Sn, Br, Cl, Cs in [0,1].

Task: Propose a final simplified quadratic structure:
- Allowed: constant + a subset of main effects + a small subset of squares + a small subset of key pairwise interactions
- Not allowed: any cubic+ terms, ratios, logs, exponentials

Hard constraints:
1) Minimize complexity: target 8–11 non-constant terms total.
2) Every retained term must have clear physical meaning (Sn doping, halide mixing, A-site stabilization/lattice effect, and key couplings).
3) Prefer terms likely to generalize: strong main effects + only a few essential couplings (e.g., Sn–halide, Cs–B/X).
4) Do NOT provide numerical coefficients and do NOT explain.

Output format: exactly one line, the final expression structure.
